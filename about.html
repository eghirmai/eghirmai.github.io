A bit about me

Hi, my name is Eva. I'm from Seattle and live in DC. I work in the humanitarian space on data initiatives - thinking about how we can use data to respond better to disasters. Before then, I did international development research to help organizations and governments make better policy decisions. I think a lot about our moral obligations to others.

Outside of work, I spend my time on photography, design, and music. I play jazz occasionally, but these days I mostly listen. I drink a lot of coffee, cycle around DC, and try to hack my way through life with behavioral science research.
